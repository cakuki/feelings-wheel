## What's original here

- **All artwork** is generated by this repository's own code (`gen_wheel.py` → SVG).
  Nothing is traced, scanned, or derived from anyone else's image.
- **All word lists** (core emotions and nuanced feelings, in every language) are
  this project's own selection and translations.

## The concept (prior art we credit, not copy)

The idea of arranging emotions as a wheel — core emotions in the center radiating
out to more nuanced feelings — is a long-established *concept*, originated in the
academic literature:

- **Robert Plutchik**, *Wheel of Emotions* (1980).
- **Gloria Willcox**, *The Feeling Wheel*, Transactional Analysis Journal, 12(4),
  274–276 (1982).

A concept, system, or method is not protected by copyright (only a specific
creative *expression* is). We credit these originators for the idea, but our core
emotion set (Happy, Surprised, Angry, Scared, Sad, Calm), our nuanced-feeling
vocabularies, and every pixel of the artwork are our own. We do not reproduce
either author's chart.

"Feelings wheel" is used here as a plain descriptive term for this kind of tool,
not as a brand.
